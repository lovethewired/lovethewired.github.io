Consensus Mechanisms

Why do we need consensus?

Reaching consensus is important to be able to resist sybil attacks and to agree on the current valid state of the blockchain. More formally, consensus is reached when 66% of the nodes agree on the network state.

What is a sybil attack?

It's a form of attack on a network through an entity that creates multiple fake identities. Sybil resistance is gained if a network is able to resist such an attack. PoW's resistance comes through the expenditure of a limited resource: compute power. PoS's resistance comes through the expenditure of a limited resource: capital. PoHumanity also conceivable.

What is Proof of Stake?

PoS is a consensus mechanism by which validators are selected by their capital

  • less energy consumption
  • lower barrier of entry
  • reduced centralization risk (ASICs, specialized miners)
  • less ETH issuance required
  • higher security due to high deterrents (slashing)

How are validators chosen?

PoS on ethereum requires locking 32 ETH in deposit contract and run execution client, consensus client, and a validator. Validators receive blocks from peers.

What is a fork?

A fork is when a blockchain is split into multiple divergent blockchain paths. The result is two or more current blockchain heads. And more generally, when parties are not in agreement on the true state of the network.

This can happen due to:

  • consensus failure (failure in validator client implementation)
  • dishonest behavior
  • network latency

also intentional:

  • social agreement (DAO hack)
  • upgrades

PoS fork-choice rule LMD GHOST.

Hard fork:

  • new blocks validated under old software would be deemed invalid

Soft fork:

  • old nodes might accept blocks that appear invalid under the new rule

How are forks resolved (fork choice rule)?

Through consensus mechanisms. I.e. under PoW the longest chain is selected. In PoS the 'heaviest' chain is selected (most attestations weighted by stake). Can also be resolved through social consensus or software upgrades.

What is block finality?

A transaction can reach finality once it cannot change without a significant economic burden.

In **PoW finality is probabilistic - after a certain amount of blocks it becomes very unlikely to be undone -, in PoS it is through Casper FFG?

In PoS finality is managed using checkpoint blocks (first block in each epoch = 32 blocks). Validity of checkpoint blocks are voted on in pairs. Earlier block becomes "finalized", more recent block becomes "justified".

  • requires 2/3 of total staked ETH.
  • attacker could prevent this with 1/3, but inactivity leak burns away eth of inactive validator
  • no reward if validators fail to respond, minor penalty for not submitting votes
  • slashing for dishonest behavior (signing two blocks in one slot): 0.5 eth on day 1, correlation pen. on day 18, ejection on day 36

What is a 51% attack? What can happen?

A 51% attack is when miners collude that control over 50% of the network's mining hash rate. They are then able to:

  • Prevent other miners from completing blocks (censor)
  • Reverse transactions (reorgs)
  • Double spends (affects exchanges)

51% attacks can also occur when a bug in the software exists, allowing a malicious user to produce faster blocks (ethereum classic, or bug in Verge XVG blockchain april 2018).

Generally requires a lot of resources: electricity, mining servers, storage space, legal risk.
