I just heard about the 2022 Underhanded Solidity Contest and submissions are due in a day.

About the Contest

From the website: "The goal of the Underhanded Soldity Contest is to write seemingly innocent and straightforward-looking Solidity code which actually contains malicious behavior or backdoors."

I have recently started getting into Solidity CTF challenges and wanted to give it a go. Designing smart-contract challenges sounds like a fun task. There have been times while coding a contract where I had to shudder, because I realized a terrible mistake in my code. I thought some of these could end up being interesting CTF Challenges.

This years theme for the challenge is about decentralized exchanges. Any kind, orderbook or AMM. And, "simplicity is key" is emphasized.

I came up with two challenges that I uploaded to Github if you want to give them a try!

I was first going to go with submitting the bitmap challenge "ExclusiveExchange.sol", because it came across as unique (albeit also a bit of a niche setting). Although, after stripping away any unnecessary, distracting fluff from the contract, the vulnerability seemed too obvious. And, especially after hearing that @samczsun from paradigm will be on the board (famous for his pinball machine - probably one of the hardest challenges regarding bitmaps), I decided to come up with another one.

So, I wrote the second challenge "NFTOrderBook.sol". The setup for this one is a bit more realistic and the bug is more common, although a bit simple. I still have not seen any other CTF challenge revolve around this vulnerability/error, so it could be a good fit.

I might have a small write-up, after I have some people test them out and give me feedback.
